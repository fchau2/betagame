Q1
Component Knowledge
Choose two components from the list below and explain in your own words what each one does inside a computer. Use an example to support each answer.
4 marks2–3 sentences per component
⚡ CPU 🧩 RAM 💾 Storage (HDD/SSD) 🎨 GPU 🔋 PSU ❄️ Cooling
Think about what the component stores, processes or manages. For example — the CPU processes every instruction the computer runs, similar to how a brain processes thoughts. Without a CPU, the computer can't do anything at all.
Q2
Component Knowledge
Explain the difference between RAM and Storage (HDD/SSD). Why does a computer need both? What would happen if a computer had lots of storage but very little RAM?
4 marks3–4 sentences
RAM is your computer's short-term memory — fast but temporary. Storage is long-term memory — slower but permanent. Think of RAM as your desk (what you're working on right now) and storage as your filing cabinet (everything saved for later).
Q3
Customer Matching
Look at the two customers below. For each one, identify the most important component they need and explain why that component matters more than the others for their use case.
4 marks2 sentences per customer
Mrs. Thompson
Primary school teacher — writes reports, emails and spreadsheets. Budget of $600.
Jake
16-year-old gamer — wants to play the latest games at high settings. Budget of $1,200.
Mrs. Thompson's work is not graphics-intensive — she doesn't need a dedicated GPU. Focus on what makes her PC reliable and affordable. Jake needs to render real-time 3D graphics at high frame rates — think about which component is responsible for that.
Q4
Customer Matching
A hospital needs a new workstation PC with a budget of $2,500. List three specific requirements you would prioritise and explain why each one matters in a medical environment where the PC must never slow down or crash.
6 marks2 sentences per requirement
St. Mary's Hospital
Radiology department — runs medical imaging software on multiple monitors, handles patient databases, critical reliability required.
Think about: what happens if the PC freezes mid-scan? What does imaging software need to run smoothly across multiple monitors? How much RAM do you need to keep large patient files open? Would an HDD be fast enough for a hospital database?
